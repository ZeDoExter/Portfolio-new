For a decade, we stared at white voids. "Clean" design. "User-centric" emptiness. But 2026 demands more. 

The layout you are looking at right now—a throwback to the tactile, ink-stained layouts of the 20th century—is part of a larger movement. We crave texture. We crave friction. We want to feel the digital paper grain under our fingertips.

Users are reporting higher engagement rates on sites that look "busy." The theory is that in an age of AI-streamlined content, human messiness feels authentic. It feels trustworthy.
