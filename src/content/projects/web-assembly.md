In a stunning turn of events, WebAssembly (Wasm) has officially surpassed native JavaScript execution speed for complex heavy-lifting tasks in the browser. 

The new "Białystok Protocol" introduced by the W3C last month allows for zero-copy memory sharing between the GPU and Wasm threads, effectively turning the browser into a high-performance gaming console.

"It's not just about games anymore," says Dr. Elena Krovoc, lead researcher at The Institute of Digital Archaeology. "We are seeing financial modeling, weather simulations, and even neural network training happening right inside the tab, with no lag."

Developers who stuck to pure JS are now scrambling to learn Rust and Zig, as the demand for Wasm-native modules increases by 300% quarter-over-quarter.
